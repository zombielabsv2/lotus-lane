Daisaku Ikeda on Prayer & Faith

Guidance on the power of prayer, daimoku, and sincere faith as the engine of transformation in Nichiren Buddhism.

15 quotes

“Prayer is the courage to persevere. It is the struggle to overcome our own weakness and lack of confidence in ourselves.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, For Today and Tomorrow

“A powerful prayer — one that is the roar of a lion — can change anything, any situation, no matter how difficult or seemingly impossible.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The New Human Revolution, Vol. 3

“Buddhism teaches that prayer without action is just wishful thinking, and action without prayer lacks the power to break through fundamental darkness.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The Wisdom for Creating Happiness and Peace

“When you chant with a deep, unwavering determination, you activate the protective functions of the universe that work to support your efforts.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, Faith Into Action

“Faith is about taking action. It is about challenging yourself. It is about not being defeated. That is what true faith means.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, Discussions on Youth

“The purpose of faith is to become happy — not to become a slave to rigid rules or formalities.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The Wisdom for Creating Happiness and Peace

“Do not let your prayer become a mere formality. Infuse it with your deepest desires and your sincerest determination.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, For Today and Tomorrow

“Earnest prayer — the kind where your whole being trembles with the desire to break through — will without fail be answered.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The New Human Revolution, Vol. 5

“To chant when you do not want to chant is the spirit of a lion. It is the spirit that transforms karma.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, Youth and the Writings of Nichiren Daishonin

“Buddhism is reason. If prayers go unanswered, there is always a reason. Faith means having the courage to look for that reason within your own life.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The Wisdom for Creating Happiness and Peace

“Faith is the ultimate source of strength. When you have faith, you can turn any situation around.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, For Today and Tomorrow

“Your daimoku is like a sword that cuts through all obstacles. But you must wield it with conviction.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, Discussions on Youth

“Prayer is not about begging or pleading. It is about resolving — making a powerful determination — and then acting on that determination.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The New Human Revolution, Vol. 11

“The essence of faith is the continual challenge to grow, to develop, and to become the very best version of yourself.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, The Wisdom for Creating Happiness and Peace

“Even if you feel your prayers are not being answered, they are. Every sincere prayer reaches the universe. Sometimes the answer just takes a different form than we expected.”

— Daisaku Ikeda, For Today and Tomorrow
