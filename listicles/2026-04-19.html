When Life Feels Dark: 5 Ancient Reminders That Light Is Already on Its Way

April 19, 2026 · Hope
1.
“Winter always turns to spring. Never, from ancient times on, has anyone heard or seen of winter turning back to autumn.”
- Daisaku Ikeda, The Writings of Nichiren Daishonin (referenced in guidance)

Whatever painful season you are living through right now, history itself guarantees it cannot last forever.

2.
“Hope is not a matter of ability; it is a matter of decision.”
- Daisaku Ikeda, Discussions on Youth

Feeling hopeful has nothing to do with your circumstances - it is a choice you can make even on your worst days.

3.
“No matter how hopeless or bleak things appear, the moment you resolve to never give up, every nerve and fiber in your being will orient itself toward your success.”
- Daisaku Ikeda, Faith Into Action

A single firm decision to keep going can physically shift how your mind and body respond to the obstacles in front of you.

4.
“No one can make you feel hopeless unless you allow them to. Hope resides in the heart. It is something you create.”
- Daisaku Ikeda, Discussions on Youth

The people or situations draining your hope only have that power if you forget that hope is something you generate from within.

5.
“Each morning we are born again. What we do today is what matters most.”
- Daisaku Ikeda, For Today and Tomorrow

No matter how badly yesterday went, today is a clean reset - a real, practical chance to start differently.
